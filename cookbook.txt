[Spaghetti with Meat Sauce]
Ingredients:
    1 pound ground beef
    1 large onion, chopped
    1 green bell pepper, chopped (optional)
    2 cloves garlic, minced
    1 (28 ounce) can crushed tomatoes
    2 (6 ounce) cans tomato paste
    1/2 cup water
    2 teaspoons dried oregano
    1 teaspoon dried basil
    1 teaspoon salt
    1/2 teaspoon black pepper
    1 pound spaghetti

Instructions:
    1. Combine ground beef, onion, garlic, and green pepper in a large saucepan over medium-high heat. Cook and stir until meat is browned and crumbly and vegetables are tender, 5 to 7 minutes. Drain grease.
    2. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, water, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer. Reduce heat to low, cover, and cook for at least 30 minutes, stirring occasionally. The longer it simmers, the richer the flavor.
    3. Meanwhile, bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti in the boiling water until al dente, about 8 to 12 minutes. Drain.
    4. Serve meat sauce over cooked spaghetti.

[Baked Salmon with Roasted Asparagus and Quinoa]
Ingredients:
    4 salmon fillets (about 6 oz each)
    1 bunch asparagus, tough ends trimmed
    1 cup quinoa
    2 cups vegetable or chicken broth (or water)
    2 tablespoons olive oil, divided
    1 lemon, sliced
    Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
    Optional: fresh herbs like dill or parsley for garnish

Instructions:
    1. For the Quinoa: Rinse quinoa thoroughly under cold water. In a medium saucepan, combine rinsed quinoa and broth (or water). Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes, or until all liquid is absorbed. Let stand, covered, for 5 minutes, then fluff with a fork.
    2. For the Salmon and Asparagus: Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
    3. Place the salmon fillets on the baking sheet. Arrange the asparagus around the salmon. 
    4. Drizzle 1 tablespoon of olive oil over the salmon and asparagus. Season generously with salt and pepper. Place lemon slices on top of the salmon.
    5. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until salmon is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork, and asparagus is tender-crisp. Cooking time will vary based on salmon thickness.
    6. Serve the baked salmon and roasted asparagus alongside the fluffy quinoa. Garnish with fresh herbs if desired.

[Lentil Soup]
Ingredients:
    1 tablespoon olive oil
    1 large onion, chopped
    2 carrots, chopped
    2 celery stalks, chopped
    2 cloves garlic, minced
    1 (14.5 ounce) can diced tomatoes, undrained
    1 cup brown or green lentils, rinsed
    6 cups vegetable broth
    1 teaspoon dried thyme
    1/2 teaspoon dried rosemary
    Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
    Optional: fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:
    1. Heat olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add onion, carrots, and celery; cook until softened, about 5-7 minutes. Add garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
    2. Stir in diced tomatoes, rinsed lentils, vegetable broth, thyme, and rosemary. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until lentils are tender.
    3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
    4. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley if desired.
[Lemon Herb Roasted Chicken with Roasted Root Vegetables]
Ingredients:
    - 1 whole chicken (3-4 lbs)
    - 2 lemons, one sliced, one for juice
    - 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
    - 2 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
    - 2 tbsp fresh thyme, chopped
    - 1/4 cup olive oil
    - 1 lb carrots, peeled and chopped
    - 1 lb potatoes, chopped (such as Yukon Gold or red potatoes)
    - 1 large onion, roughly chopped
    - Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Instructions:
1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
2. In a small bowl, combine olive oil, minced garlic, chopped rosemary, chopped thyme, the juice of half a lemon, salt, and pepper.
3. Pat the chicken dry with paper towels. Rub the herb mixture all over the chicken, including under the skin if desired. Stuff the cavity with lemon slices.
4. On a large baking sheet, toss the chopped carrots, potatoes, and onion with a drizzle of olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread them in an even layer.
5. Place the seasoned chicken on top of the vegetables.
6. Roast for 1 hour to 1 hour and 15 minutes, or until the chicken\'s internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C) in the thickest part of the thigh and the vegetables are tender and lightly caramelized.
7. Let the chicken rest for 10-15 minutes before carving. Serve with the roasted root vegetables.

[Speedy Shrimp Scampi with Linguine]
Ingredients:
    - 1 lb linguine
    - 1.5 lbs large shrimp, peeled and deveined
    - 1/4 cup olive oil
    - 4 tbsp unsalted butter
    - 6-8 cloves garlic, minced
    - 1/2 cup dry white wine or chicken broth
    - Juice of 1 lemon
    - 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
    - 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
    - Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
    - Grated Parmesan cheese, for serving (optional)

Instructions:
1. Cook linguine according to package directions in a large pot of salted boiling water. Before draining, reserve 1 cup of pasta water.
2. While pasta cooks, heat olive oil and 2 tablespoons of butter in a large skillet over medium heat.
3. Add minced garlic and red pepper flakes (if using) and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant (do not brown the garlic).
4. Add shrimp to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, until pink and opaque. Remove shrimp from the skillet and set aside.
5. Pour in the white wine (or chicken broth) and lemon juice into the skillet. Bring to a simmer and cook for 2-3 minutes, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan.
6. Stir in the remaining 2 tablespoons of butter until melted.
7. Return the cooked shrimp to the skillet. Add the cooked and drained linguine, chopped parsley, and about 1/2 cup of the reserved pasta water. Toss everything together, adding more pasta water if needed to create a light sauce.
8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
9. Serve immediately, garnished with grated Parmesan cheese if desired.

[Hearty Lentil Soup with Crusty Bread]
Ingredients:
    - 1 tbsp olive oil
    - 1 large onion, chopped
    - 2 carrots, peeled and diced
    - 2 celery stalks, diced
    - 4 cloves garlic, minced
    - 1 (14.5 oz) can diced tomatoes, undrained
    - 1 cup brown or green lentils, rinsed
    - 6 cups vegetable broth
    - 1 tsp dried thyme
    - 1/2 tsp dried rosemary
    - 1 bay leaf
    - Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
    - Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish (optional)
    - Crusty bread, for serving

Instructions:
1. Heat olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add chopped onion, carrots, and celery. Sauté for 8-10 minutes, or until vegetables are softened.
2. Add minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
3. Stir in the diced tomatoes (with their juice), rinsed lentils, vegetable broth, dried thyme, dried rosemary, and bay leaf.
4. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until the lentils are tender.
5. Remove the bay leaf. Season the soup with salt and pepper to taste.
6. For a thicker soup, you can mash a portion of the lentils against the side of the pot or use an immersion blender to partially blend the soup.
7. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley if desired, alongside plenty of crusty bread.

[Sheet Pan Sausage and Bell Peppers]
Ingredients:
    - 1.5 lbs Italian sausage (sweet or hot), cut into 1-inch pieces
    - 3-4 bell peppers (various colors), cored and sliced into strips
    - 1 large red onion, sliced into wedges
    - 2 tbsp olive oil
    - 1 tsp dried oregano
    - 1/2 tsp garlic powder
    - Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
    - Optional: 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes

Instructions:
1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a large sheet pan with parchment paper for easy cleanup.
2. In a large bowl, combine the Italian sausage pieces, bell pepper strips, and red onion wedges.
3. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with dried oregano, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Toss until all ingredients are well coated. Add cherry tomatoes if using.
4. Spread the mixture in a single layer on the prepared sheet pan.
5. Roast for 25-30 minutes, or until the sausage is cooked through and the vegetables are tender and slightly caramelized, stirring halfway through.
6. Serve hot, perhaps with a side of rice, pasta, or crusty bread.

[Build-Your-Own Pizza Night]
Ingredients:
    - **For the Dough (or store-bought pizza crusts/naan bread):**
        - 1 packet (2 1/4 tsp) active dry yeast
        - 1 tsp sugar
        - 1 cup warm water (105-115°F / 40-46°C)
        - 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
        - 1 tsp salt
        - 2 tbsp olive oil, plus more for greasing
    - **For the Sauce:**
        - 1 (15 oz) can crushed tomatoes
        - 1 clove garlic, minced
        - 1/2 tsp dried oregano
        - Salt and pepper to taste
    - **Cheese:**
        - 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
        - 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
    - **Topping Suggestions (choose your favorites!):**
        - Pepperoni, cooked sausage, ham, grilled chicken
        - Mushrooms, onions, bell peppers, black olives, spinach, jalapeños, pineapple
        - Fresh basil, red pepper flakes

Instructions:
1. **If making dough:** In a large bowl, combine warm water, yeast, and sugar. Let sit for 5-10 minutes until foamy.
2. Stir in olive oil, salt, and gradually add flour, mixing until a shaggy dough forms.
3. Turn out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic.
4. Lightly oil a clean bowl, place the dough in it, turning to coat. Cover with plastic wrap and let rise in a warm place for 1-1.5 hours, or until doubled in size.
5. **For the sauce:** In a small bowl, combine crushed tomatoes, minced garlic, oregano, salt, and pepper. Set aside.
6. **Prepare toppings:** Chop and prepare all your chosen toppings and arrange them in separate bowls for easy assembly.
7. Preheat oven to 450°F (230°C) with a pizza stone or baking sheet inside if you have one.
8. Once dough has risen, punch it down gently. Divide into desired number of pizzas (e.g., two medium pizzas).
9. On a lightly floured surface, roll out each dough ball into your desired thickness and shape. Transfer to a piece of parchment paper or a cornmeal-dusted pizza peel.
10. Spread a thin layer of sauce over the dough, leaving a crust border. Sprinkle with mozzarella and Parmesan cheese. Arrange your chosen toppings.
11. Carefully transfer the pizza to the preheated oven (onto the pizza stone/baking sheet).
12. Bake for 10-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly and lightly browned.
13. Remove from oven, let cool for a few minutes, slice, and serve immediately.

[Classic Beef Burgers with Homemade Sweet Potato Fries]
Ingredients:
    - **For the Burgers:**
        - 1.5 lbs ground beef (80/20 for best flavor)
        - 1/2 tsp salt
        - 1/4 tsp black pepper
        - 4 hamburger buns
        - Optional: 4 slices cheddar or American cheese
        - Lettuce leaves, tomato slices, onion slices, pickles for serving
        - Ketchup, mustard, mayonnaise for serving
    - **For the Sweet Potato Fries:**
        - 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into 1/4-inch thick fries
        - 2 tbsp olive oil
        - 1 tsp cornstarch (optional, for crispier fries)
        - 1/2 tsp paprika
        - 1/4 tsp garlic powder
        - Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Instructions:
1. **Prepare Sweet Potato Fries:** Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a large baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. In a large bowl, toss the sweet potato fries with olive oil, cornstarch (if using), paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper until evenly coated.
3. Spread the fries in a single layer on the prepared baking sheet. Ensure they are not overlapping to encourage crispiness.
4. Bake for 20-30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until golden brown and crispy. Keep an eye on them as cooking times can vary.
5. **Prepare Burgers:** While fries are baking, gently mix ground beef with salt and pepper in a bowl (do not overmix). Form into 4 equal patties, making a slight indentation in the center of each to prevent bulging.
6. Heat a large skillet or grill over medium-high heat. Cook burgers for 4-5 minutes per side for medium-well, or to your desired doneness. If adding cheese, place a slice on each patty during the last minute of cooking and cover to melt.
7. Toast hamburger buns lightly on the grill or in a separate pan.
8. Assemble burgers: Place cooked patty (with melted cheese if using) on a toasted bun. Add your desired toppings and condiments.
9. Serve hot with the homemade sweet potato fries.

[Slow Cooker Pot Roast with Carrots and Potatoes]
Ingredients:
    - 3-4 lbs chuck roast or similar cut
    - 1 tbsp olive oil
    - 1 large onion, chopped
    - 4 cloves garlic, minced
    - 1 lb carrots, peeled and cut into large chunks
    - 1.5 lbs Yukon Gold or red potatoes, scrubbed and quartered (or left whole if small)
    - 1 (10.75 oz) can condensed cream of mushroom soup (or cream of celery/chicken)
    - 1 packet (1 oz) dry onion soup mix
    - 1 cup beef broth
    - 1 tsp dried thyme
    - Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
    - Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish (optional)

Instructions:
1. Season the chuck roast generously with salt and pepper.
2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear the roast on all sides until nicely browned (about 3-4 minutes per side). This adds flavor.
3. Place the chopped onion, minced garlic, carrots, and potatoes in the bottom of your slow cooker.
4. Place the seared roast on top of the vegetables.
5. In a medium bowl, whisk together the condensed cream of mushroom soup, dry onion soup mix, beef broth, and dried thyme until well combined.
6. Pour the soup mixture evenly over the roast and vegetables in the slow cooker.
7. Cover and cook on LOW for 7-8 hours, or on HIGH for 3-4 hours, until the roast is fork-tender and easily shreds.
8. Once cooked, you can remove the roast and shred it with two forks, then return it to the slow cooker with the vegetables and sauce.
9. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.
10. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley if desired.
